Maker spotlight

Hitohira trail

New source-led additions.

Hitohira adds brand, retailer and story context: Jiro, Morihei, Moki, Daitoku, Tadokoro, Takada, Tetsujin and related source trails.

Sakai · Sharpener / PolisherMitsuaki Takada

Thin convex hamaguri-style grinding, Suiboku finish and modern Sakai polish.

Known lines: Takada Suiboku, Takada Reika, Takada Ginsan

Sakai · BlacksmithKenji Togashi

Sakai blacksmith frequently discussed around honyaki, carbon forging and high-end retailer lines.

Known lines: Togashi honyaki, Togashi White #1, Togashi Blue #1

Sakai · SharpenerTadokoro Hamono

Sakai sharpening and finishing house, represented here with Morihiro student-lineage context and collaborations with named smiths; the wide-bevel deep dive describes Tadokoro as the sturdier, food-release-leaning branch of the comparison.

Known lines: Tanaka x Tadokoro, Tadokoro Ginsan, Tadokoro Blue #1

Sakai · SharpenerKyuzo / Yauchi

Sakai wide-bevel sharpening trail kept separate from Myojin/Izo; the Reddit deep dive frames Kyuzo as the more aggressive, high-performance hollow-grind side of the Morihiro-student comparison.

Known lines: Tanaka x Kyuzo, Nakagawa x Kyuzo, Togashi x Kyuzo

Sakai · BrandHitohira

Curated Japanese knife brand where hidden and named smith/sharpener pairings matter.

Known lines: Tanaka x Kyuzo, Togashi x Kyuzo, Nakagawa x Morihiro

Sakai · Blacksmith / BrandGenkai

Sakai honyaki context and Hitohira-listed brand trail for higher-end traditional work.

Known lines: Genkai honyaki, Hitohira Genkai

Sanjo · BlacksmithShigefusa

Tokifusa Iizuka and sons in Sanjo; benchmark hand work with Swedish carbon steel, sen finishing, kasumi and in-house Kitaeji cladding.

Known lines: Shigefusa Kitaeji, Shigefusa Kasumi, Shigefusa Western

Tosa / Kochi · Sharpener / PolisherNaohito Myojin

High-end convex sharpening and polishing; Myojin and Izo are treated here as buyer-facing names for the Naohito Myojin profile.

Known lines: Myojin Riki, Tetsujin, Myojin Cobalt Special

Tosa / Kochi · BrandTetsujin

Buyer-facing line identity where smith and polisher attribution matter.

Known lines: Tetsujin Metal Flow, Tetsujin kasumi, Tetsujin Blue #2 Kasumi

Seki / Gifu · Workshop / BrandMoki

Seki/Gifu production brand listed by Hitohira, with outdoor, pocket and other edge tools alongside stainless steel product lines.

Known lines: Moki pocket knives, Moki fixed blades, Moki steak knife

Nagano · Blacksmith / BrandJiro

Low-volume Jiro Tsuchime knives with Hitohira retailer-routing context and a BBQ knife story tied to mountain cooking in Nagano.

Known lines: Jiro Tsuchime Gyuto, Jiro Tsuchime Sujihiki, Jiro BBQ Knife

Mie · BlacksmithDaitoku

Hitohira-listed blacksmith story: a fifth-generation family context, old-style local-order work and hand-finished edges, with bread knife work highlighted.

Known lines: Daitoku bread knife, Daitoku forged knives
